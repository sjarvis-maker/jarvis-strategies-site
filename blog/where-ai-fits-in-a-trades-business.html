Where AI Actually Fits in a Trades Business

Scott Jarvis  ·  May 13, 2026  ·  5 min read

Every week another AI tool promises to transform your business. Most of them were built by software developers who have never set foot on a job site. The result is a lot of expensive technology that doesn't map to how a trades business actually operates.

After 18 years in the trades and now working with Okanagan companies on AI implementation, I've found the same pattern every time: there are three or four places where AI genuinely saves time and money, and a dozen places where it creates more work than it replaces.

Here's where the real opportunities are.

Estimating and quoting

This is the highest-value use case for most trades businesses. A good AI-assisted estimating workflow can cut quote turnaround from days to under two hours — I've seen it happen. The key is feeding it your historical job data so it learns your actual costs, not industry averages.

What works: AI that pulls from your past quotes, flags scope creep risks, and generates a first draft you then review and adjust. What doesn't work: AI that generates quotes from scratch with no historical grounding. You'll win jobs you lose money on.

Client communication

Responding to quote requests, sending project updates, following up on outstanding invoices — these tasks eat hours every week and none of them require your expertise. They require consistent, professional communication.

AI handles this well. A template-based system with AI-generated draft responses, reviewed before sending, keeps clients informed without pulling you off the tools. The review step is non-negotiable — AI doesn't know your client relationships.

Scheduling and dispatch

This one is more complex. AI scheduling tools work well when your jobs are predictable in scope and duration. They work poorly when every job is different and half of them run long. Know which category you're in before investing here.

Where AI doesn't fit

Site supervision, quality control, and anything requiring physical judgment. AI cannot look at a rough-in and tell you if it's right. Don't let anyone sell you on that yet.

The question isn't "can AI do this?" It's "does AI doing this make my business better or just more complicated?"
